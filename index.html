The Heart
Is this what your heart looks like?

Mostly. What you can see are the left and right ventricles, which are the main pumping chambers of the heart. There are two other chambers (not shown), called the left and right atria, which are located above the ventricles. The atria receive blood from the body and pump it into the ventricles.
What is the heart’s job?

The purpose of the heart is to pump blood around the body to provide all the organ systems with essential oxygen and nutrients, and to take away the waste.
Can you describe what the heart is doing?

Did you see it get smaller and larger? Contraction of the heart muscle squeezes the blood inside the ventricles.

Did you see the pressure trace increase as the heart gets smaller? This increase in pressure drives the blood around the body.

The heart is filled with blood again when it relaxes.
What are the main things that can go wrong with the heart?

Heart attacks, electrical fibrillation, and heart failure.
Heart Attack
 
Does the heart muscle need blood?

Yes, the heart muscle walls need blood to survive. The heart pumps blood to itself through vessels called the coronary arteries. Unhealthy lifestyle choices can cause narrowing of these arteries in what is called a heart attack.

What could happen if a coronary artery narrowed?

If a coronary artery narrows then less oxygen and nutrients can reach the muscle. This can cause damage of the heart muscle, which can lead to a minor or severe heart attack.
Heart Electricity
Healthy
What makes the heart beat?

If you have ever had an electric shock you may have noticed that it made your muscles tense up. In a similar way, electricity causes contraction of the muscular walls of the heart.

Coordinated electrical waves trigger smooth contractions, which are needed for effective pumping in the healthy heart.
What happens when the smooth electrical waves are disrupted?

The electrical waves become random and the heart muscle just wobbles in a state called fibrillation.
Heart Failure
 
Can the heart wear out?

Yes. Long term problems such as high blood pressure or untreated heart attacks can eventually cause the heart to wear out. This is called heart failure.
What happens during heart failure?

The shape, stiffness, and contraction of the heart are abnormal and the heart needs to work harder than normal to meet the demands of the body.

In the early stages of heart failure, the heart can compensate for these problems. However, if left untreated, this can lead to decompensated heart failure, during which it can no longer pump enough blood.
Heart Attack
Minor
What causes a heart attack?

Bad lifestyle choices can cause fatty deposits to build up inside the coronary arteries, obstructing the blood flow.

What changes can you observe in the heart after a heart attack?

Did you see that the darker part did not contract as much? (Double tap to look inside.) Did you notice the pressure drop?

If the heart cannot contract very well then it’s ability to develop pressure and pump the blood is reduced.

If left untreated, this can lead to a severe heart attack.
Heart Failure
Compensated
What changes can you observe in the heart?

Did you see that the walls are thicker? (Double tap to look inside.)

Long term heart problems such as high blood pressure or heart attacks require the heart to work harder. The muscular walls of the heart compensate for this higher load by becoming thicker.
Why should you treat heart problems as early as possible?

If heart problems are left untreated, eventually the heart muscle will no longer be able to cope. This leads to decompensated heart failure.
Heart Attack
Severe
What would happen if a large blockage obstructed blood flow?

If a coronary artery gets very narrow, then even less blood can get through, which results in even more muscle damage. The movement and pumping of the heart is severely affected.

Heart Electricity
Fibrillation
What happens during fibrillation of the heart?

Did you notice the dramatic change to the ECG? Did you notice the large pressure drop?

The electrical waves in the heart become chaotic, which means that the heart does not contract smoothly and cannot generate enough pressure to pump the blood.
What can we do to fix a fibrillating heart?

We need to use an Automated External Defibrillator (AED) to apply a large electrical shock, which resets the electrical waves.

Heart Failure
Decompensated
What changes can you observe in the heart?

Did you see that the heart has ballooned and the walls are thinner? (Double tap to look inside.)

By this point, the heart has worn out and the larger heart is no longer able to cope with the high work load.
What effects does decompensated heart failure have on the body?

Did you see that the pressure has decreased?

The failing heart cannot generate as much pressure to pump enough blood to meet the demands of the body. This results in symptoms such as dizziness, breathing difficulties, inability to exercise, and chest pains.
